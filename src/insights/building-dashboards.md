Most dashboards fail because they're built for stakeholders, not for decisions. Here's how we approach dashboard design at MIKAEL GLOBAL.

## The Problem

Dashboards are often designed to display data, not to drive action. They become visual clutter — beautiful but useless.

At MIKAEL GLOBAL, we take a different approach: we start with the decision, not the data.

## Our Framework

### 1. Define the Decision

What specific decision does this dashboard inform? Without a clear answer, the dashboard is not worth building.

### 2. Design for Action

Every chart, metric, and filter should guide the user toward a specific action. If it doesn't, cut it.

### 3. Test with Real Users

We prototype early and test with actual decision-makers — not with managers, but with the people who click the buttons.

## Real Results

Using this framework, we've helped clients reduce report-generation time by 60% and increase data-driven decision frequency by 2x.
